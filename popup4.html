Cappuccino

Cappuccino is a popular Italian coffee beverage known for its rich and creamy taste. It consists of three key components: espresso, steamed milk, and milk foam. Here's a description of each element: 1. Espresso: - Cappuccino starts with a strong shot of espresso, which is a concentrated coffee brewed by forcing hot water through finely ground coffee beans. This forms the robust and flavorful base of the drink. 2. Steamed Milk: - The next component is steamed milk, which is heated and frothed to create a velvety and creamy texture. The steamed milk is added in roughly equal proportions to the espresso, resulting in a balanced combination of coffee and milk. 3. Milk Foam: - Cappuccino is topped with a generous layer of milk foam. The foam is created by aerating milk, trapping tiny air bubbles to achieve a light and airy consistency. This foam not only adds a delightful visual element to the drink but also contributes to its overall texture.
