A little about of the main

The legend of humanity's acquaintance with coffee is linked to an Ethiopian goatherd named Kaldi, who noticed that his goats became more energetic after eating coffee cherries. Monks also discovered the invigorating properties of a beverage made from these cherries, and "kava" (coffee) became part of their religious rituals. Coffee spread from Arabia to Europe and other continents, becoming one of the most popular beverages in the world.

Brazil's Coffee Fields: A Global Java Haven

In Brazil, vast coffee plantations are renowned for their rich history and impact on the global coffee industry. Spread across regions like Minas Gerais, São Paulo, and Bahia, these fields benefit from diverse landscapes and climates, resulting in a wide variety of flavorful beans. Blend of tradition and innovation, with both mechanized farms and manual harvesting. Harvest season from May to September, showcasing meticulous cherry-picking and careful processing. As the world's largest coffee producer, Brazil significantly influences global coffee prices and supplies. Plantations offer immersive experiences, connecting visitors with the essence of coffee culture and craftsmanship. In essence, Brazil's coffee fields are the beating heart of a beverage that unites coffee lovers worldwide, combining history, flavor, and culture.

Masterpieces of brazilian fields

The dance of flavors begins with the meticulous harvest season, spanning from May to September. Ripe cherries are plucked with care, preserving the essence of Brazil's diverse terroirs. Through precise processing and careful sorting, these cherries transform into the beans that captivate the senses of coffee enthusiasts worldwide. Brazil, as the world's largest coffee producer, weaves its magic not only through its beans but also its influence on the global coffee market. With a history rooted in the 18th century, Brazilian coffee plantations have shaped the coffee landscape, dictating trends and setting standards for excellence.
